# Authorship, ownership, and answerability

**Owner and answerable party.** All work in this repository — code and prose — is
owned by Jérôme Vogel and authored *of record* by him. He is the sole party
answerable for every claim and every commit: the only one who can be bound by it,
who signs it, and who answers to stakeholders for it.

**AI as method, not author.** Parts of this repository were produced with AI
assistance (Anthropic's Claude, used through Claude Code) as a working instrument —
a medium and executor, not an author, owner, or answerable party. It holds no
copyright, bears no moral rights, and answers to no one. Under U.S. and Canadian law
an AI system cannot be an author; under Anthropic's terms any rights in the outputs
are assigned to the user. Where the record notes AI tooling — commit trailers, this
file — it discloses *method and provenance*, never authorship or agency.

**On the seam.** In genuinely coupled human-AI work the line of "who generated this"
is not cleanly auditable. This file therefore does not claim that a human solely
authored every expression. It claims the thing that *is* determinate: Jérôme Vogel
solely *answers* for all of it. Integrity rests not on auditing the seam but on
holding the whole coupling answerable to fixed external refusers — the archive that
corrects a misquotation, the compiler that refuses bad logic, and the git record that
cannot be silently rewritten.

**Commit history.** On 2026-07-06 the commit history of this repository — and of its
sister repositories — was rewritten to strip the `Co-Authored-By: Claude` trailers,
which linked to Anthropic's own GitHub account (`github.com/claude`) and credited it
as a repository contributor. Only commit messages were changed; every file tree is
byte-identical, so no work was altered. Going forward, AI assistance is disclosed with
an instrumental `Generated-with:` trailer, and the human remains the sole recorded
author and answerable party.
